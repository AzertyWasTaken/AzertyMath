Exponentiation

Definition

Exponentiation is an operation that indicates how many times a number is multiplied to itself.

Exponentiation is denoted with the ^ symbol.

Properties

Exponentiating any number to 1 never change its result.

Exponentiating any number to 0 always equal 1.

Exponentiating 1 to any number always equal 1.

Exponentiation is not commutative: changing the order of its numbers may change the result.
