Prime factorization uniqueness

Statement

Every positive integers have a unique prime factozation.

Proof

Let \(n\) be the smallest integer with 2 distinct prime factorisations:

\(n = \left\{ p_1 \times p_2 \times ... \times p_k \right\} = \left\{ q_1 \times q_2 \times ... \times q_l \right\}\)

If some number \(p_i\) equals some number \(q_j\), they cancel themselves from both sides to get a smaller number with two different prime factorizations â€” contradicting minimality of \(n\). Hence no \(p_i\) equals any \(q_j\).

Take the prime number \(p_1\). Since \(n\) is divisible by \(p_1\), a number \(q_j\) must be also divisible by a number \(p_1\), which is impossible since all numbers \(q_j\) are prime.
