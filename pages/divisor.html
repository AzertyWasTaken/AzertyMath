Divisor

Definition

An integer is divisible by another integer if its division have no remainder.

A divisor of an integer \(n\) is an integer \(m\) such as \(n\) is divisible by \(m\).

The divisor count of the first \(n\) numbers are \(1, 2, 2, 3, 2, 4, 2, 4, 3, 4\).

Properties

The fraction of an integer \(n\) and an integer not divisible by \(n\) result in a rational number.

An integer \(n\) is divisible by an integer \(m\) if \(n \div m\) have no remainder.

Every integer is divisible by \(1\) and itself.

A number have a odd number of divisors if and only if it is square.

If an integer \(n\) is a divisor of an integer \(m\), \(m \div n \) is also a divisor of \(m\).
