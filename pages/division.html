Division

Definition

Division is an operation that involve separating a number into equal parts: it is the inverse of multiplication.

Division is denoted with the \(\div \) symbol.

The result of division is called the fraction.

Example

If you divide \(12\) to \(4\), you get \(3\).

That is \(12 \div 4 = 3\).

Properties

  • Division is not commutative:

    • \(a \div b \neq b \div a\)

  • The identity of division is \(1\):

    • \(n \div 1 = n\)

  • 0 divided by any number equal \(0\):

    • \(0 \div n = 0\)

  • Any number (except \(0\)) divided by itself equal \(1\):

    • \(n \div n = 1\)
