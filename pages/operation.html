Operation

Definition

An operation is a process that takes two numbers and form a third one called the result.

The four basic operations are addition, subtraction, multiplication and division.

Properties

An operation is commutative if changing the order of its numbers does not change the result.

The identity of an operation is a number \(n\) such as any number \(m\) and \(n\) equal \(m\).

Order of operations

The order of operations is a set of rules that tells us the correct order to do steps in a calculation.

  1. Brackets
  2. Exponents
  3. Multiplication and division
  4. Addition and subtraction

Brackets are denoted with symbols \((\) and \()\).
