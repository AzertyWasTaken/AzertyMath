Triangular number

Definition

The triangular sequence is a sequence where the \(n\)th number is the sum of the first \(n\) positive integers.

The \(10\) first triangular numbers are \(1, 3, 6, 10, 15, 21, 28, 36, 45, 55 \).

The formula of triangular number is \(n \times (n + 1) \div 2 \).

\(3\) is the only prime triangular number.
