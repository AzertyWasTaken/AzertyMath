Prime number

Definition

A prime number is an integer that have exactly 2 divisors.

They cannot be written as the product of two smaller integers greater than 1.

Numbers that are not prime are called composite numbers.

The first ten prime numbers are 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, 17, 23, 29, 31.

Properties

  • 2 is the only even prime number.
  • There exist infinitely many prime numbers.
  • Every integers greater than 1 can be written as a product of prime numbers.
  • If \(a \times b\) is divisible by a prime number \(p\), \(a\) or \(b\) is divisible by \(p\).
