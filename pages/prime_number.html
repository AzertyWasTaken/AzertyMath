Prime number

Definition

A prime number is an integer that have exactly \(2\) divisors.

Prime numbers cannot be written as the product of two smaller positive integers.

Numbers that are not prime are called composite numbers.

The first ten prime numbers are \(2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, 17, 23, 29, 31\).

Properties

  • \(2\) is the only even prime number.
  • There exist infinitely many primes.

Proof

Assume there are only finitely many primes.

Let \(P\) be a list of these prime numbers.

Let \(n\) be the product of each number in \(P\) plus \(1\).

\(n\) is not divisible by any number in \(P\) because their division have a remainder of \(1\).

So \(n\) is either a prime number itself or a multiple of a prime number that do not appear in \(P\).
