Number

Definition

Numbers are ideas we use to show “how many” or “how much” of something there is.

They are used to count, measure and label.

Types of number

Positive number

Positive numbers are numbers greater than 0.

Example: \(1, 2, 5, 13, ...\)

Negative number

Negative numbers are numbers less than 0

They are denoted with a \(-\) in the beginning of the number.

Example: \(-2, -8, -11, ...\)

Rational number

Rational numbers are numbers that can be expressed as the fraction of .

Rational number can also be negative.

They are written on the form \(\frac{p}{q}\).

Example: \(\frac{1}{2}, 5, \frac{-4}{7}, ...\)

Integer

Integers are numbers that has no fraction — just whole steps on the number line.

They can be negative, zero, or positive, but never “in between” like \(\frac{1}{2}\).
