Number

Definition

A number is an idea we use to show “how many” or “how much” of something there is.

Types of number:

Positive number

A positive numbers is a number greater than \(0\).

Example: \(1, 2, 5, 13, ...\)

Negative number

A negative number is a number less than \(0\)

They are denoted with a \(-\) in the beginning of the number.

Example: \(-2, -8, -11, ...\)

Rational number

A rational number is the result of a number \(n\) divided by another number which is not a divisor of \(n\).

Rational number can be also negative.

They are written on the form \(\frac{p}{q}\).

Example: \(\frac{1}{2}, \frac{2}{3}, \frac{-4}{7}, ...\)

Integer

An integer is any number that is not rational.

Sign

The sign of a number is the property of being positive or negative.

\(0\) is the only number that is neither positive or negative.
