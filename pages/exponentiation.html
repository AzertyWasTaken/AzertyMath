Exponentiation

Definition

Exponentiation is an operation that indicates how many times a number is multiplied to itself.

This operation is denoted \(b^n\).

Properties

  • Exponentiation is not commutative:

    • \(b^n \neq n^b \)

  • The identity of division is \(1\):

    • \(b^1 = b \)

  • \(0\) exponentiated to any number (except \(0\)) equal \(0\).

    • \(0^n = 0 \) if \(n \neq 0\)

  • \(1\) exponentiated to any number equal \(1\).

    • \(1^n = 1 \)

  • Any number exponentiated to \(0\) equal \(1\).

    • \(b^0 = 1 \)

  • \(b^{n + m} = b^n \times b^m \)
  • \({(b^a)}^b = b^{a \times b} \)
  • Prime factorizations of a number \(b^n\) always come in multiples of \(n\).
