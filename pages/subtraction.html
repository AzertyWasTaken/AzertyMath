Subtraction

Definition

Subtraction is the way we find out what number is left when we take some away.

This is one of the four basic operations.

Subtraction is denoted with the \(-\) symbol.

The result of Subtraction is called the difference.

Example

    If you have \(5\) candies 🍬🍬🍬🍬🍬 and you eat \(2\) 🍬🍬, you’re left with \(3\) candies 🍬🍬🍬.

    That is \(5 - 2 = 3\).

Addition can also be thought as repeated predecessions.

\(5 - 2 = P(P(5))\).

Where \(P(n)\) is the predecessor operation.

Properties

  • Subtraction is not commutative:

    • \(a - b \neq b - a\)

  • The identity of subtraction is \(0\):

    • \(n - 0 = n\)

  • Any number subtracted to itself equal \(0\):

    • \(n - n = 0\)
