Subtraction

Definition

Subtraction is an operation that remove a number to another number: it is the inverse of addition.

Subtraction is denoted with the \(-\) symbol.

The result of Subtraction is called the difference.

Example

If you subtract \(2\) to \(5\), you get \(3\).

That is \(5 - 2 = 3\).

Properties

  • Subtraction is not commutative:

    • \(a - b \neq b - a\)

  • The identity of subtraction is \(0\):

    • \(n - 0 = n\)

  • Any number subtracted to itself equal \(0\):

    • \(n - n = 0\)
