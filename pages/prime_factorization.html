Prime factorization

Definition

The prime factorization of an integer is the decomposition of a positive integer into a product of prime numbers.

Every positive integers have a prime factozation.

Example

20 can be written as the product of 4 and 5 \((4 \times 5)\).

4 can be written as the product of 2 and 2 \((2 \times 2)\).

So the prime factorization of 20 is \(2 \times 2 \times 5\).

To make it shorter, we use exponents, like \(2^2 \times 5\).

Properties

  • 1 has no number in its prime factorization.
  • Every positive integers have a unique prime factozation.
  • Prime factorizations of square numbers always come in pairs.
  • Prime factorizations of a number \(b^n\) always come in set of \(n\).
