Prime numbers infinitude

Statement

There exist infinitely many prime numbers.

Proof

Assume there are only finitely many prime numbers.

Let \(P\) be a list of these prime numbers.

Let \(n\) be the product of each number in \(P\) plus 1.

\(n\) is not divisible by any number in \(P\) because their division have a remainder of 1.

Therefore, \(n\) is either a prime number itself or is a multiple of a prime number that do not appear in \(P\).
