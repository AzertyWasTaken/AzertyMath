Square number

Definition

The square sequence is a sequence where the \(n\)th number equal \(n\) multiplied by itself.

The first ten square numbers are 1, 4, 9, 16, 25, 36, 49, 64, 81, 100 .

Properties

  • The \(n\)th square number equal \(n^2\).
  • A number is square if and only if it have a odd number of divisors.
  • 1 and 36 are the smallest number that is both square and triangular.
  • Prime factorizations of square numbers always come in pairs.
  • Numbers on the form \(n \times (n + 1)\) and \(n \times (n + 2)\) are not square.
  • The product of a square number and a non square number is not square.
  • The product of two square numbers is a square number.
  • The \(n\)th square number is semiprime if and only if \(n\) is prime.
